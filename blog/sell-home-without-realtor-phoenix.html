Step 2: Price Your Phoenix Home Competitively

Pricing strategy is critical in Phoenix's varied market:

  • Spring (Feb-May): Peak selling season — price at or slightly above comparable sales
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): Slower season — price aggressively to attract buyers
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): Moderate activity — competitive pricing works best
  • Winter (Dec-Jan): Snowbird season — cash buyers active, price for quick action

Use these Phoenix-specific resources:

  • Maricopa County Assessor records for square footage and lot size
  • ARMLS sold data (provided by FSBOOS)

Yard sign strategy for Phoenix:

  • Place signs early morning or evening (avoid midday heat)

Step 5: Handle Phoenix Showings

Showing practices for Phoenix's climate:

  • AC set to 72°F — buyers won't linger in a warm house
  • Schedule evening showings in summer months (June-September)
  • Provide cold water bottles at open houses
  • Turn on ceiling fans for air circulation
  • Showcase outdoor spaces in cooler months

Phoenix FSBO Paperwork Requirements

Maricopa County and require specific disclosures:

  • Seller Property Disclosure Statement (SPDS): Detailed property condition disclosure
  • Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: Required for homes built before 1978

Frequently Asked Questions: Phoenix FSBO

How long do FSBO homes take to sell in Phoenix?

In the current Phoenix market, well-priced FSBO homes with MLS exposure sell in 14-25 days. Homes priced above market can sit for 60+ days.

Do I need to offer buyer agent commission in Phoenix?

Yes, strongly recommended. Offer 2.5-3% to buyer agents to ensure your home gets shown. Phoenix buyers predominantly work with agents.

What's the best time to sell FSBO in Phoenix?

February through May is peak season. September through November is also strong. Avoid listing in July-August unless you need a quick sale.

Can I sell FSBO in a Phoenix HOA community?

Yes, but obtain HOA documents early. Many Phoenix HOAs have resale packages that take 5-10 days to prepare. Buyers need these for financing.

How do I handle multiple offers on my Phoenix FSBO?

Multiple offers are common in hot Phoenix neighborhoods. Evaluate: price, financing type, earnest money, contingencies, and closing timeline. Cash offers often win even at lower prices.
