FSBO Arizona FAQ

Is it legal to sell my home without a realtor in Arizona?

Yes, absolutely. Arizona law allows homeowners to sell their own property (For Sale By Owner). You're required to provide specific disclosures (SPDS), but FSBOOS provides all necessary forms and guidance.

Will buyer agents show my FSBO home?

Yes. When you list on the Arizona Regional MLS through FSBOOS, buyer agents see your listing just like any other. We offer the standard 2.5-3% buyer agent commission, so agents are incentivized to bring buyers.

How long does it take to sell a home without an agent in Arizona?

In Arizona's current market, FSBO homes sell in an average of 18-25 days — similar to agent-listed homes. The key is proper MLS exposure and competitive pricing, both of which FSBOOS provides.

What do I need to disclose when selling by owner in Arizona?

Arizona requires a Seller Property Disclosure Statement (SPDS) and Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (for homes built before 1978). FSBOOS provides all required forms and instructions.

Can I cancel my FSBO listing if I change my mind?

Yes, you can cancel your FSBOOS listing at any time with no penalties. However, we recommend giving it at least 30 days for full market exposure.

Do I need a real estate attorney in Arizona?

Not required, but recommended for complex transactions. FSBOOS provides contract templates that comply with Arizona law. For most standard sales, our support is sufficient.
